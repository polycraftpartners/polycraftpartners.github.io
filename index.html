Frequently Asked Questions

What you are probably wondering.

What is a fractional commercial director?
A fractional commercial director is a senior commercial executive who works with your company part-time rather than as a full-time hire. You get the same strategic capability, market knowledge, and relationship management as a CCO — without the $200,000+ annual salary and overhead. PolyCraft operates as a fractional commercial director for B2B companies entering or growing in new markets, typically on a monthly retainer with a 90-day minimum.
How long does it take to see results?
A Commercial Market Entry project typically runs 8 to 12 weeks and delivers a qualified pipeline and initial relationships by the end of that period. The honest expectation is that Months 1 and 2 are foundation-building, and Month 3 is where the first tangible commercial outcomes begin to appear. Any partner who promises revenue in week one is not being honest with you.
Do you work with companies outside the US and UK?
Yes. PolyCraft works with companies from any geography that are entering the US, UK, Ireland, France, MENA, or Central and Eastern European markets. A Tunisian company entering France, a French company entering the UK, a US company entering the Gulf — all are within PolyCraft's direct experience and capability.
What makes PolyCraft different from a sales agency?
Sales agencies measure activity — calls made, emails sent, appointments booked. Their incentive is volume. PolyCraft operates as a commercial partner with accountability for the revenue trajectory. PolyCraft brings market intelligence, senior relationship management, and commercial architecture — things a volume-based SDR team cannot provide regardless of how many calls they make.
What does it cost a company to delay this decision?
More than the engagement fee. Every quarter without senior commercial direction is a quarter where deals stall, competitors build relationships you have not started, and the founder remains the bottleneck. The cost of inaction in a new market is not zero — it is compounding. Markets reward companies that move with structure. They punish the ones that move without it.
